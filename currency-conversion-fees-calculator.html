What Are Hidden Currency Conversion Fees?

Your bank doesn't tell you the real fee. Instead, they quote you a conversion rate that's worse than the actual market rate. This "markup" or "spread" is their hidden profit. You think you're getting the fair rate — you're not.

Example: The real AUD/USD rate (mid-market) is 0.68 today. Your bank quotes you 0.66 — that's 0.02 (0.29%) worse. On a $10,000 AUD transfer, you lose $290.

Why Banks Hide Their Fees

  • It's not advertised: Banks don't send you a bill for the markup. It's baked into the rate they give you.
  • It looks official: The rate appears "quoted" — you assume it's fair because it's from a bank.
  • You can't compare easily: Without knowing the mid-market rate, you have no way to tell if you're being overcharged.
  • Customers don't ask: Most people don't realize there's even a hidden fee — they just accept what the bank offers.

How to Calculate Your Real Currency Conversion Cost

  1. Find the mid-market rate: Use ConverterPro to check the real rate (mid-market). This is what the market actually trades.
  2. Get a quote from your bank: Call your bank and ask for their conversion rate on the exact amount you want to transfer. Write it down.
  3. Calculate the difference: (Bank rate - mid-market rate) / mid-market rate × 100 = your markup %
  4. Multiply by your amount: Transfer amount × your markup % = dollars lost

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I'm being overcharged on currency conversion?

Compare your bank's quoted rate to the mid-market rate (use ConverterPro to check). If your bank's rate is worse by 1-4%, you're being overcharged.

What is a "mid-market" currency rate?

The mid-market rate is what the currency actually trades at between banks, right in the middle between the buy and sell prices. It's the fairest rate.

Are all currency conversion fees the same?

No. Banks charge 2-4% hidden markups. Wise, OFX, and Remitly charge 1-2% transparent fees. Big difference on large amounts.

Can I avoid currency conversion fees entirely?

No service is free. But using Wise (1.5-2%) instead of your bank (2-4% + wire fees) saves you 50-75% on every transfer.
