Frequently Asked Questions

How does the sleep calculator work?

Sleep happens in 90-minute cycles. Waking at the end of a cycle feels natural and refreshed. The calculator adds multiples of 90 minutes to your bedtime (plus 14 min to fall asleep) to show the ideal wake times.

How many sleep cycles do I need?

Most adults need 5–6 sleep cycles per night (7.5–9 hours). The minimum is 4 cycles (6 hours). 3 cycles or fewer will leave you tired.

Why do I feel groggy after 8 hours?

8 hours doesn't align to 90-min cycles — it falls mid-cycle. Try 7.5 hours (5 cycles) or 9 hours (6 cycles) instead.

What is a sleep cycle?

A 90-minute block of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM (dream) sleep. Your brain repeats this all night. Waking mid-cycle causes "sleep inertia" — that groggy, heavy feeling.
