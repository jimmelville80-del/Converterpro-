Banks don't advertise hidden fees. Here's what you're really paying:

Your Bank 2-4% markup + $10-50 wire fee $200-450
Western Union 3-8% + flat fee $300-850
Wise 1-2% transparent fee $100-200

  4. Make the transfer: Use Wise, OFX, or similar service with a transparent fee.

What's a "Mid-Market Rate"?

The mid-market rate is the real exchange rate used between banks—it's the fairest rate available. Most banks hide this and add 2-4% markup. Services like Wise use the mid-market rate and charge a small transparent fee instead of a hidden markup.

Ways to Minimize Conversion Costs

  • Use Wise or OFX for transfers: They charge transparent fees on the real mid-market rate.
  • Avoid PayPal for currency exchange: PayPal charges 2.5-3% markup + 1.5% fee = too expensive.
  • Don't use airport exchange kiosks: They charge 10-20% markups. Withdraw cash from ATMs instead.
  • Check rates before traveling: Some credit cards have lower foreign transaction fees (1-2% vs 3-4%).
  • Bundle transfers: One large transfer costs less per dollar than multiple small transfers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are hidden currency conversion fees?

Banks add a 2-4% markup (called a spread) on top of the real exchange rate. This is charged silently without transparent disclosure on most transfers.

How much do banks charge for currency conversion?

Typically 2-4% markup plus wire fees ($10-50). On a $10,000 transfer, that's $200-400+ in hidden costs.

Can you really convert currency without fees?

You can't eliminate fees entirely (providers need to stay in business), but Wise charges transparent ~1.5-2% fees with no markup—that's the lowest available.

Is Wise cheaper than Western Union?

Yes, significantly. Wise charges ~1.5-2% with the real rate. Western Union charges 3-8% plus high fees, making it 2-4x more expensive.
